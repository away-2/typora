FlexiSpot Washable Rug Buyer’s Guide
Designed for practicality, a washable, non-slip rug ensures easy maintenance, lasting durablity, and added safety, making it the perfect choice for homes with kids, pets, or busy lifestyles.
What to Consider When Shopping for a Washable Rug？
Size and Fit
Ensure the rug fits your space and your washing machine. Larger rugs may require a detachable design for easier machine washing.
Ease of Maintenance
Look for features like anti-slip backing, detachable components, and a smooth surface design that makes vacuuming effortless while simplifying cleaning and reassembly.
Style and Design
Choose a design and color that complement your home decor, from traditional patterns to modern styles.
Durability
Choose rugs made from durable, stain-resistant materials like polyester or microfiber, designed to withstand high-traffic areas, repeated washing, and regular cleaning without losing their texture, color, or integrity.
Safety Features
Check for anti-slip or gripper backing to keep the rug securely in place and reduce tripping hazards. Additionally, ensure the rug is made from eco-friendly, non-toxic materials for a safer home environment.

Rug Cleaning Tips
Machine Washing Tip
Remove the rug from the rug pad before washing. Before cleaning, please select the appropriate washing machine based on the rug size shown on the right.
Machine-wash the rug in cold water with the patterned side facing outward. Do not use detergents containing bleach.

Drying Tip
We strongly recommend hanging the rug outside to dry. (The rug dries quickly.) If hanging is not possible,dry on low heat. Do not use fabric softeners.
Vacuum Cleaning Tip
Vacuum on the lowest setting and avoid using industrial vacuums with brushes. Be careful not to vacuum the edges of the rug.

Rug Pad Cleaning Tips
Do not machine-wash the rug pad. Instead, hand-wash them and hang them outside to dry.
1. Use a damp cloth with soapy water for spot cleaning.
2. Dry the pad by rubbing it with a towel.
Rug Gripper Cleaning Tips
Make sure rug grippers do not go into the washing machine. If the grippers lose their stickiness, simply rinse them with water and place them outside to dry before reusing them.
