Does the office chair suit VARIOUS BODY TYPES?
According to our research, we have found that different office chairs are suitable for different body types, so you need to take some measurements before buying.
A. Seat Height Measurement: Measure the height from your knee to your feet, ensuring your feet are flat on the ground when seated. Your thighs should be parallel to the floor.
B. Seat Depth Measurement: Measure the distance from your thigh to your knee. The seat depth should be such that 3 or 4 fingers(Greater than or equal to 50mm) can pass between the front edge of the seat and the lower leg.
In addition to this, the lumbar support of the office chair should match the natural lumbar curvature of the user’s back. The arm height should be such that the forearm can easily reach them but not be in the way when using a keyboard or mouse.

What adjustment points are included in FlexiSpot's office chair？
We have 10 adjustment points:
1. 
Seat Height Adjustability: The seat height ought to allow users to sit with their feet flat on the ground and a minimal amount of weight placed on the backs of the legs. , thighs horizontal, and arms even at the height of the desk.
2. 
Seat Depth and Width Adjustability: The seat’s depth can be moved forward or backward, depending on the needs of the user. It should be adjusted so that the back of the chair does not press against the calves.
3. 
Seat Tilt: Seat Tilt: The angle of the seat should be such that you feel most comfortable in an upright posture. The seat tilt can be adjusted to provide an angle that counterbalances good posture: feet flat, back comfortably resting on the chair’s back. Adjust as needed to create a seating surface that gently pulls you back to the chair as you sit in a strong, upright posture.
4. 
Seat Tilt: The armrests should be adjustable in height and width, allowing the user’s arms to rest comfortably and shoulders to remain relaxed.
5. 
Headrest Adjustability: The headrest can be angled to the user’s ideal position, it should be adjustable to accommodate different heights and neck lengths.
6. 
Lumbar Support Adjustability: The ability of lumbar support is a critical feature designed to provide the correct support for the natural curve of your spine, particularly the lower back, or lumbar region. Good lumbar support can help to prevent back strain and muscle fatigue, allowing you to sit for longer periods while maintaining good posture.
7. 
Back Recline: This feature allows you to adjust your sitting angle. Most ergo chairs will allow for between 90 and 120 degrees of tilt, though some will let you recline all the way back. With a good back recline feature, you should be able to sit in a position that is comfortable for you and supports the natural curvature of the spine. A back recline function is especially important for people who work at the desk for extended periods of time as it helps to reduce the pressure on your spine and lower back.
8. 
Tilt Tension: Tilt tension control determines the amount of resistance you’ll feel when leaning back in your chair. By adjusting the tilt tension, you can control the ease or difficulty of reclining in your chair, allowing for comfortable and controlled reclining. The perfect tilt tension will give you a sense of weightlessness and allow you to move easily in the chair.
9. 
Syncro Tilt: A more advanced feature found in some chairs, this enables the seat and backrest to tilt simultaneously while maintaining the same angle. This can be beneficial for maintaining postural alignment and distributing weight evenly.
10. 
Swivel: The chair should be able to swivel freely to allow for easy access to various areas of the workstation.
